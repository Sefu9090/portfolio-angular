Problem Statement

When discussing what our group wanted to focus on we came across the conversation of accessiblity, that is when we knew we wanted to design a tool that will help poeple with mobility impairments get around. MobilityMaps is a mobile app designed to aid individuals with mobility disabilities by providing them with local navigation routes that utilizes crowdsourcing techniques to eliminate unneeded and unforeseen obstacles from their routes.

Competitive Analysis

Our competitive analysis revealed where we wanted to aim for as our market base. Compared to other top competing navigation apps, our goal was to capitalize on both routing technologies and crowdsourcing practices in order to provide accurate, real time information about routes and destinations to all users.

Research and Insights

Academic Research

Recognized the potential of crowdsourcing in order to recognize obstacles on sidewalks and pathways. Lack of ADA compliance, crowds, construction, steep inclines, stairs, uneven terrain, etc. all hindered pedestrian navigation across a wide variety of disability types, especially for those with mobility disabilities. Participants indicated that they use many techniques when searching online out of necessity, as the information is not always readily available.

Vlogs and Blogs

  • “A 5 minute walk for a non disable user tends to turn out to be an adventure for a user with mobility disability"
  • “Having the need to go into the street due to an uncrossable sidewalk pavement”
  • “Some slopes are just too steep”

Personas

After researching mobility limitations, my group identified four main groups that we decided to focus on. Of these groups I was tasked with creating personas for poeple with crutches. People might be using crutches for many reasons, not just because of age, which is why I decided to create personas for both young users that might have an injury and an older adult that uses crutches for moving around.

Wireframe

From the research data my group collected, we then put together sketches of what we believed could solve some of the problems people with mobility limitations were facing. For my sketch/wireframe I decided to go with a potential map view in which the user would see color coded overlays on the map of areas that they might want to avoid and a corresponding legend on the left.

User tests

Good

  • Customizing routing options on first page
  • Ability to report obstacles at the time of encounter
  • Clean, and easy to understand UI

Bad

  • Non - smooth surfaces are trouble
  • Include Temporary vs permanent obstacles
  • Following ADA guidelines
  • Providing images of locations interior can be very helpful
  • Needed more explicit symbols

Changes

  • Simplified location rating system
  • Removed redundant route options
  • Increased photo gallery options
  • Differentiated restroom icons that have accessible options and those that do not

User Tests allowed us to get a view into what an actual user, who we hoped would daily drive the app, wants our app to do for him. From the test we were able to see what worked, what did not work, and what needed to be added. Being able to get actual insight into the needs of our customers was a great benefit in the development of our app. In the end we came out with a positive outlook of our project as the user said we had a great idea and would like to see it properly excecuted.

Prototype

The final Prototype of our application has gone through many trials and revisions. It has became something that we are proud to show and it is demonstration on why the need for accessiblity is so important.
